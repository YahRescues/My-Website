Let My People Go

Then the LORD said to Moses, “Go to Pharaoh and tell him that this is what the LORD says: ‘Let My people go, so that they may worship Me. But if you refuse to let them go, I will plague your whole country with frogs
Exodus 8:1-2

God's people were enslaved in Egypt. They were being hindered from serving God. He says let My people go so that they may worship me, or He would bring judgement on the system which enslaved His people and kept them from truly worshipping Him. Today we have the same thing, the world system is anti-God. It doesn't merely hinder God's people from truly worshipping Him, it is no longer a hinderance. A hinderance would be the bus was late but it still came. Today the bus isn't late, it just isn't showing up. What I mean is this: It is no longer you can read the Bible, believe it, and pray, but you will be mocked. That would be a hinderance. Now it is you can't believe the Bible, you aren't allowed to speak what the Bible says on certain matters, you can't pray because you have to do X, Y, and Z instead. Nobody has a gun to your head saying you can't pray, but the world system won't allow you time to pray. So, regardless if they have a gun to your head or not, the world system is keeping you from praying and having time all alone to yourself with God. It is keeping you from raising your kids in a godly environment with biblical standards and morals. It is only so long before God says enough is enough, just like He did in Exodus. This is the work God does in us. He works to free us from slavery to sin, so that we will be free to be a slave to Him. After salvation satan is going to come and work to free us from slavery to God, and bring us back into slavery to sin. We all have a choice, after the delivery from Egypt, Israel still were told to choose for themselves who they would serve.

Now, therefore, fear the LORD and serve Him in sincerity and truth; cast aside the gods your fathers served beyond the Euphrates and in Egypt, and serve the LORD. But if it is unpleasing in your sight to serve the LORD, then choose for yourselves this day whom you will serve, whether the gods your fathers served beyond the Euphrates, or the gods of the Amorites in whose land you are living. As for me and my house, we will serve the LORD!” Joshua 24:14-15

Many times, Israel made the wrong choice, but God was merciful, and when they turned back to God, He turned back to them. But when they turned their back on God, He turned His back on them. Meaning when they prayed He wasn't looking upon them to favour them, His face wasn't shining upon them, they didn't have the light of His countenance, but only cursing and darkness.

This goes right back to what was said at the beginning. We have become strangers and aliens in our nation. True believer today should feel like a stranger in a foreign land. If prior generation could arise and see our nation they wouldn't recognise it. We have become slaves to the world system. You can't function as a citizen without technology. You usually need a phone number for work, sometimes you might even need social media. These are the kind of things which are keeping God's people from a fuller revelation of God. Not having a phone number itself, but needing to keep contant tabs on these things isn't good. The end result is that God came with judgement to that nation which enslaved His people and they worshipped Him. In this time God is being merciful, though we don't realise, we are living in a time of mercy. God will judge by sending no prophet, but still He is being merciful, allowing people to repent and giving us times of ease. Many things we pray for, God will not answer. His response would be the same as it was to Jeremiah if he prayed for God to unjudge what he had brought judgement upon. Many times, we pray to God and ask Him to unjudge what he has brought judgement upon. We live under the consequences of sin and rebellion against God, when God brings judgement and we don't like the consequences, we pray that He would undo what He has done and is doing. Israel could have prayed that God wouldn't send the plagues, but he would have sent them anyway. Even when Moses prayed and God had mercy, it only resulted in a further plague later. We can pray that God would heal our nation, but if our nation keeps doing the things which bring the judgement of God it will become sick again. A nation must let Gods people go that they may serve him. Our nations today are keeping God's people in bondage, keeping them from serving God. God will bring judgement on whatever keeps His people from serving Him. What if a flood took away access to electronics? What if there was no internet connection where you are? There is no security outside of Gods shelter, and He will do whatever He has to do to bring His people back to Him. He did whatever He had to do so that Pharaoh let His people go that they may serve Him, and He will do whatever he has to do so that our leaders, and the false gods let us go so that we may truly serve God how He should be served. Change is coming and everything which can be shaken will be shaken, everything which can be removed will be removed.
