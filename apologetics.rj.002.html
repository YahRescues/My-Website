No blood No atonement

For the life of the flesh is in the blood, and I have given it to you to make atonement for your souls upon the altar; for it is the blood that makes atonement for the soul.
Leviticus 17:11

Indeed, according to the law nearly everything is purified with blood, and without the shedding of blood there is no remission.
Hebrews 9:22

There is no forgiveness without the shedding of blood. The Torah states that the blood is given to make atonement. The sacrificial system became impossible in 70AD with the destruction of the temple. Why did God allow this to happen if the only way of remission is through blood? What blood has been available to atone for sins for almost 2000 years? An objection to this is that when Jonah preached the people repented, and were forgiven without blood. But this doesn’t deal with Leviticus, it makes the matter worse because there are now two discrepancies. The answer to the question is that the suffering servant is about a man. This man had to come before 70AD in order for there to be remission through blood. Messiah was prophesied to be killed in Daniel. Isaiah agrees with this in saying the suffering servant was cut off from the land of the living. The reason that the people of Nineveh could be forgiven is revealed in Romans 3:21-26. God could look to the cross and the blood atonement which would occur in the future. This is why blood sacrifices were accepted. It is not that the animals themselves were capable of taking away sins, and the guilt of sins. But they pointed to the one to come, and God could look into the future and see the one to come, accepting the blood sacrifices of bulls and goats. God could Passover His people when He saw the blood of a lamb. It was not because of how much He likes Lamb blood, but He could look into the future and see what this pointed to. However, if this is not the case then it must be explained how God has been forgiving sins for almost 2000 years without blood, and how He forgave other people when they repented without shedding blood.
